## In focus

### The Joy of Being Disabled Explore what it means to be joyful, defiant and disabled. Guest edited by Amy Kavanagh, this series of five articles celebrates different bodies and minds, and challenges ableist ideas of what it means to be happy.

[### Books We publish books that explore health and human experience. Our latest title, ‘The Coming of Age: Notes on Getting Older’, is an anthology on ageing, bringing together science, myth and essays by Angela Saini, Sharon Blackie and Venki Ramakrishnan.](https://wellcomecollection.org/books)

## Latest stories

* Article ### Representing women's experiences of infertility From medieval birth scrolls to online discussion forums, Sally Butcher's artwork explores the the historical and contemporary impact of language on how we think about (or don't think about) infertility. Part of Protection and the medieval birth scroll

* Book extract ### Through the valley of gloom What does depression feel like – and what helps? Gavin Francis reflects on language, culture and care, and the small steps that can light the way through the gloom. Part of Inside our books

* Long read ### The art of intensive care If we can imagine a utopian future for medicine, its model would be the ICU, where the holistic care of each unwell person is the cornerstone of life support. Elinor Cleghorn explores the history of intensive care, and how her brush with death taught her care’s true meaning.

* Article ### Protecting body and soul in medieval childbirth Dr Róisín Donohoe examines the place of the birth scroll, in protecting women in childbirth from death, loss and damnation. Part of Protection and the medieval birth scroll
