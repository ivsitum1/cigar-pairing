**The Davidoff canon (excerpt from *Connoisseur's Book of the Cigar*, 1967, summarised on Wikipedia, *Cigar*, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigar):**

> Rules of cigar etiquette:
> 1. Never light a cigar in a hurry; let the flame linger.
> 2. Smoke the cigar only halfway through.
> 3. Let the cigar go out by itself; never stub it out.
> 4. The band may be removed once the cigar has cooled.
> 5. Never relight a cigar more than two-thirds smoked.
> 6. The finer the cigar, the less one should drink.
> 7. Cigar ash should be left intact as long as possible.
> 8. Smoke the cigar in peace and quiet.
> 9. Choose a cigar for the moment — morning, day, evening.
> 10. A cigar is not a cigarette; draw gently, never inhale.
> 11. The cigar is the connoisseur's sign of maturity and good taste.
> 12. Never offer a cigar to a lady; let her ask.
> 13. The art of cigar smoking is the art of living slowly.

(Paraphrased — consult the 1967 book, https://archive.org/details/connoisseursbook0000davi, for verbatim text and page numbers.)

> "Credited with the invention of the desktop humidor, with introducing French Bordeaux nomenclature to the cigar trade through his 1946 Château series, and with persuading Cuba's state tobacco monopoly to roll a personal house brand for him at the El Laguito factory in Havana — the same atelier that produced Fidel Castro's Cohibas." — Wikipedia, *Zino Davidoff* (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zino_Davidoff).

## 5 pairing drinks with cigars

Compiled from the pairing guides above and Cigar Aficionado / Cigar Club pairings:

**Whisky**
- Light-bodied cigars → blended Scotch, Irish whiskey, lighter bourbon (Maker's Mark).
- Medium-bodied → Islay single malt (Lagoulin, Ardbeg), Speyside (Macallan), Highland (Glenmorangie).
- Full-bodied → peated Islay (Laphroaig), cask-strength bourbon, rye.

**Rum**
- Light cigars → white rum cocktails (Mojito, Daiquiri).
- Full-bodied cigars → aged dark rum (Zacapa, Diplomatico, Ron Barceló Imperial).

**Cognac / Brandy**
- Cigar Cognac tradition: Hennessy, Rémy Martin, Hine. Pair with medium-to-full bodied cigars (e.g., Cohiba, Davidoff Grand Cru).

**Port, Madeira, Sherry**
- Vintage Port with Maduro wrappers.
- Pedro Ximénez sherry with dark, sweet cigars.
- Oloroso sherry with earthy cigars.

**Coffee / Espresso**
- Cuban espresso with robusto cigars; Italian espresso with darker maduros.

**Tea / Cocoa**
- Earl Grey or jasmine tea with mild Connecticut cigars.
- Dark hot chocolate with full-bodied Nicaraguan cigars.

**Beer**
- Stout/porter with maduro cigars.
- Belgian tripel with medium-bodied cigars.
- IPA with spicy cigars.

**Non-alcoholic**
- Water, sparkling water, espresso, hot black tea, dark chocolate, club soda with lime.
