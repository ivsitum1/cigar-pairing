### Smoking-related passages (verified verbatim from PG text)

**Chapter V — "On the Street and in Public" (man with cigar in mouth while talking to a lady)**

> "No well-bred man walks along the street with a lady while smoking a cigar or cigarette, although he may walk beside her smoking a pipe. ## 4 zino davidoff a guide to cigar etiquette 1967 and the connoisseur s book of the cigar 1969

### Status and licensing

- **"Zino Davidoff's Guide to Cigar Etiquette"** — original 1967 essay. Source is unclear; widely re-published and paraphrased in cigar-industry sites (Cigars International, Cigar Aficionado, Davidoff brand site). The original 1967 publication appears to be a Davidoff-brand customer pamphlet; no public-domain full text is available.
- **Zino Davidoff, *The Connoisseur's Book of the Cigar*** (Le Livre du Connaisseur du Cigare), originally published Geneva, 1967; English edition McGraw-Hill, 1969. **In copyright** — © Davidoff / McGraw-Hill. **Out of print**; secondhand copies €30–€200 on AbeBooks / antiquarian market.

### Rules as paraphrased across the cigar-industry corpus (consistent, with identical wording across sources — therefore cite as "as cited in"):

1. **Don't light up in someone's face.** Always ask permission, especially around non-smokers, in offices, in vehicles.
2. **Never use another's cigar to light your own.** "Always use a match or a cedar spill, never another cigar."
3. **Smoke slowly.** "Roughly one draw per minute. A cigar is not a cigarette."
4. **Never relight a cigar more than two-thirds smoked.** "A gentleman never relights a cigar that has more than two-thirds smoked."
5. **Don't smoke while walking.** "The cigar is meant to be savoured at rest."
6. **Don't wet the tip or "test" it with your tongue.**
7. **Don't ash constantly.** "Tap off the ash when it is at least an inch long."
8. **Do not smoke during a toast.** "If a toast is proposed, lay the cigar aside."
9. **Don't blow smoke in anyone's face.**
10. **Don't smoke in mixed company if a lady objects.**

Sources for the paraphrase (consult for full quotation context): Cigars International "Cigar Etiquette" (https://www.cigarsinternational.com/cigar-101/cigars-101/cigar-etiquette.html); Cigar Aficionado; Davidoff brand history pages; Wikipedia *Cigar etiquette* article (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigar_etiquette); archived reference at "everything.explained.today/Cigar_etiquette".

### Fair-use quotable excerpts (Wikipedia, copyleft / CC BY-SA compatible wording may be paraphrased)

> "Rules of cigar etiquette were published in 1967 by Swiss tobacconist Zino Davidoff in his essay entitled *Zino Davidoff's Guide to Cigar Etiquette*. The essay calls for cigar aficionados to do such things as smoke the cigar only halfway, let it burn out on its own, never ask another smoker for a light, refrain from smoking while walking, etc. Davidoff dismisses the elaborate rituals of lighting, says that removing or leaving the band are equally correct, and insists that a gentleman never relights a cigar that is more than two-thirds smoked."

— Wikipedia, *Cigar etiquette*, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cigar_etiquette (CC BY-SA 4.0; cite as Wikipedia).

### Verbatim from Wikipedia page (verified):

> "For example, in 19th century Havana, it was considered an insult to give another smoker a light from your cigar without first knocking off the ash from the cigar."

**Smoking-related excerpt (ch. on "Smoking and Spitting"):**

> "Smoking is a privilege which men enjoy in common, but it is one which a gentleman always uses with discretion.
