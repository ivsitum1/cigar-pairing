# Mala knjiga pušačkog bontona

> Radni rukopis za kasnije proširenje u pravu knjigu.

> Izvor u appu: `app/src/data/bonton.json` (HR/EN). Ovaj MD je hrvatski kanonski tekst za pisanje.


## Epigraf

Po uzoru na klasične britanske knjige bontona: kratka pravila ljubaznosti, napisana za stol s cigarom i čašom.


---

## I. Duh bontona


Bonton nije policija ukusa. To je način da drugi lakše dišu dok ti uživaš.

### Temelji

- Pažnja: prije nego zapališ, pogledaj tko sjedi pokraj tebe.
- Umjerenost: ritam koji ne pregrijava duhan i ne zamara društvo.
- Domišljatost: rezač, pepeljara i voda već su na stolu, bez improvizacije.
- Skromnost: tvoja najdraža maduro nije ispit za gosta.

### Zlatno pravilo

- Ako bi gestu primio kao ljubaznost, ponudi je. Ako bi je osjetio kao pritisak, odustani.


---

## II. Prostor i suglasnost


Dim ide dalje od dobre namjere. Zato prostor biraš prije nego što zapališ.

### Prije paljenja

- Pitaj, osobito u tuđem domu, na terasi blizu prolaznika, uz djecu ili u mješovitom društvu.
- Poštuj zabranu i znak. Nema pregovora o »samo jednom dimu«.
- Odmakni se od hrane koja nije dio tvoje degustacije: kuhinja, bife, tuđi desert.
- Zatvorena soba bez zraka traži kraće pušenje ili drugi trenutak.

### U društvu

- Jedan »ne, hvala« zatvara temu. Ne trebaš komentar.
- Tuđi ritam i izbor pića ne ispravljaš naglas. Savjet daješ samo ako ga zatraže.


---

## III. Nuditi i dijeliti


Kad nudiš cigaru, nudiš i vrijeme, ne samo list.

### Kako nuditi

- Ponudi izbor: blagu i srednju, ne samo svoju najjaču.
- Reci otprilike koliko traje (30, 60 ili 90 minuta), da gost zna što prihvaća.
- Rezač i upaljač idu uz ponudu. Gost ne treba tražiti alat.
- Ako odbije, ostavi cigaru mirno. Ne nagovaraj.

### Čega se kloniš

- Tuđu cigaru ne pališ bez pitanja, osim ako su rekli »upali mi«.
- Tuđe vučenje i pepeo ne ocjenjuješ naglas.
- Zadnju iz kutije domaćina ne uzimaš bez ponude.

### Dijeljenje boce

- Prvo natoči gostu. Reci što je u karafi. Voda uz stol nije dodatak, nego dio stola.


---

## IV. Tempo i prisutnost


Gospodin (i dobra družbenica) ne žuri dimom da dokaže snagu. Žuri rijetko, i samo kad mora otići.

### Ritam

- Kratka povlačenja, s pauzama. Crni vrući pepeo upozorava, ne hvali.
- Dok netko priča, razgovor ima prednost pred još jednim gutljajem dima.
- Mobitel okreni licem dolje ili ga ostavi dalje. Bonton stoljeća nije beskrajno listanje.

### Prisutnost

- Gledaj u oči kad nudiš čašu ili cigaru.
- Više slušaj nego što predaješ lekciju o XO-u.
- Ako moraš otići usred pušenja, ugasi uljudno i zahvali. Ne ostavljaj goreći ostatak kao spomenik.


---

## V. Pepeo, miris, trag


Ono što ostane iza tebe govori glasnije od imena na prstenu.

### Pepeo

- Pepeljara s utorom: ne šalica za kavu i ne rub posude s biljkom.
- Ne tresti pepeo. Pusti ga da padne. Podrži cigaru kad treba.
- Na kraju ugasi potpuno. Ako ostaješ do kraja večeri, isprazni pepeljaru ili pitaj domaćina.

### Miris

- Jako dimljeno piće i gust dim u maloj sobi najavi unaprijed, ili biraj vani.
- Parfem i kolonjsku vodu drži u mjeri. I drugi degustiraju.
- Odjeća prima dim. U zatvorenom biraj kraće formate.

### Trag

- Prsten i celofan ne idu pod stolicu. Upaljač ne ostaje otvoren na vjetru uz papir.


---

## VI. Stol: čaša, voda, redoslijed


Stol s cigarom i čašom mali je običaj, ne pozornica za egom.

### Postavljanje

- Čaša primjerena piću (snifter, Glencairn, copita), voda, pepeljara, rezač.
- Brandy ne griješ nad plamenom. Led biraj namjerno, ne iz navike.
- Natoči umjereno. Nadopuni kad gostova čaša padne nisko, bez ispitivanja.

### Redoslijed

- Miris pića, gutljaj, dim, opet gutljaj. Traži most, ne pobjedu.
- Prvo blaže, zatim snažnije, i u cigari i u čaši, kad vodiš večer.
- Ako gost zaostaje, uspori svoj ritam. Ne vuči naprijed sam.

### Riječi za stolom

- Pohvala tuđeg izbora jest bonton. Uspoređivanje »moje je bolje« nije.


---

## VII. Domaćin i gost


Dobar domaćin nudi ljestvicu. Dobar gost zna sići s nje bez srama.

### Domaćin

- Pitaj za snagu, dim i vrijeme prije nego otvoriš kutiju.
- Drži blagu i srednju cigaru te pristupačno piće (VSOP, tawny; ne peated bombu na početku).
- Pokaži gdje su pepeljara i zahod. Sitnice spašavaju večer.
- Ne forsiraj »još jednu« kad je gostu dosta.

### Gost

- Donesi mali dar ako je običaj (bocu, kutiju, ništa pretjerano).
- Poštuj vlagu i red u humidoru. Ne prekopavaj tuđe zalihe.
- Zahvali konkretno (»taj amontillado uz Connecticut…«).
- Skraćivanje zadnje trećine nije slabost. To je mjera.

### Kad nešto pođe po zlu

- Nepravilno gorenje, preljev, krivi rez: nasmiješi se, popravi tiho, nastavi razgovor.


---

## VIII. Vani i u prolazu


Vjetar i prolaznici mijenjaju pravila brže od humidora.

### Vani

- Okreni leđa vjetru. Dim ne šalji u lica za stolom.
- Jak plinski upaljač drži daleko od kose, salveta i suhog bilja.
- Nosi putnu pepeljaru ili limenku. Pepeo ne ide na travnjak restorana.
- Panatela na vjetru traži zaklon. Ne krivi format: prilagodi mjesto.

### U prolazu

- Na ulici napravi širok luk oko ulaza u zgrade i redova koji čekaju.
- U taksiju ili tuđem autu pušiš samo uz jasan pristanak.
- S balkona iznad tuđih prozora dim ide gore. Biraj sat i smjer.

### Javni prostor

- Gdje je pušenje dopušteno, i dalje vrijedi mjera. Gdje nije, gotovo je.


---

## IX. Poklon, zahvala, sjećanje


Dobar poklon otvara razgovor. Ne zatvara ga cijenom.

### Poklanjanje

- Cigare: nekoliko komada u tubi ili maloj kutiji, s bilješkom o formatu i snazi.
- Piće: boca koju bi i sam pio. Izbjegavaj »najskuplje bez konteksta«.
- Pribor: rezač ili čaša koje će koristiti, ne ukras koji skuplja prašinu.

### Zahvala

- Poruka sutradan: kratka i konkretna.
- Uzvrati pozivom kad možeš. Bonton je kružnica, ne račun.

### Sjećanje

- Zapiši što je sjelo (vitola, piće, most). Kolekcija u aplikaciji služi i tome.
- Tuđe slabosti za stolom ne pretvaraj u anegdotu vani.


---

## X. Zadnja riječ


Ako zapamtiš samo troje, neka bude ovo.

### Tri pravila stola

- Pitaj prije plamena.
- Ostavi prostor u dimu, u čaši i u razgovoru.
- Završi čistije nego si zatekao: pepeo, čaša, zahvala.

### Za kraj

- Bonton se uči kao uparivanje: bilješkom, ponavljanjem, bez glume.
- Najbolji kompliment nije »znaš pravila«, nego »ugodno mi je bilo s tobom«.


---

# English reference (app copy)


*A short book of smoking manners*


## Epigraph

In the spirit of classic British etiquette books: short rules of courtesy, written for a table with a cigar and a glass.


## I. The spirit of manners


Manners are not the police of taste. They are how others can breathe easier while you enjoy yourself.

### Foundations

- Attention: notice who sits beside you before you light up.
- Moderation: a pace that does not scorch the leaf or tire the company.
- Foresight: cutter, ashtray and water already on the table, no improvising.
- Modesty: your favourite maduro is not an exam for the guest.

### Golden rule

- If you would receive the gesture as kindness, offer it. If it would feel like pressure, let it go.


---

## II. Space and consent


Smoke travels farther than good intentions. Choose the space before the flame.

### Before lighting

- Ask, especially in someone else's home, on a terrace near passers-by, around children or in mixed company.
- Honour a ban and a sign. No bargaining for 'just one puff'.
- Keep clear of food that is not your tasting: kitchen, buffet, someone else's dessert.
- A closed room without air wants a shorter session or another moment.

### In company

- One 'no, thank you' closes the subject. No commentary needed.
- Do not correct another's pace or drink choice aloud. Advise only if asked.


---

## III. Offering and sharing


To offer a cigar is to offer time, not only leaf.

### How to offer

- Offer a choice: mild and medium, not only your strongest.
- Name the rough length (30, 60 or 90 min) so the guest knows what they accept.
- Cutter and lighter travel with the offer. The guest should not hunt for tools.
- If declined, leave it be. No coaxing.

### What to avoid

- Do not light another's cigar unasked, unless they said 'light me'.
- Do not judge their draw or ash out loud.
- Do not take the last from the host's box without an offer.

### Sharing a bottle

- Pour for the guest first. Say what is in the decanter. Water at the table is not optional; it is part of the setting.


---

## IV. Pace and presence


A gentleman (and a good companion) does not rush the smoke to prove strength. They hurry rarely, and only when they must leave.

### Pace

- Short draws, with pauses. Black hot ash is a warning, not a trophy.
- While someone is speaking, conversation outranks one more puff.
- Phone face-down or away. The manners of the century are not endless scrolling.

### Presence

- Meet the eye when you offer a glass or a cigar.
- Listen more than you lecture on XO.
- If you must leave mid-smoke, extinguish courteously and thank. Do not leave a burning stub as a monument.


---

## V. Ash, scent, and trace


What you leave behind speaks louder than the name on the band.

### Ash

- A notched ashtray: not a coffee cup, not the rim of a plant pot.
- Do not tap. Let ash fall. Support the cigar when needed.
- At the end, extinguish fully. Empty the tray if you stay through, or ask the host.

### Scent

- Heavy peat and dense smoke in a small room: announce it, or take it outside.
- Perfume and cologne in measure. Other palates are tasting too.
- Clothes hold smoke. In closed rooms prefer shorter formats.

### Trace

- Band and cellophane do not go under the chair. Do not leave a lighter open in the wind near paper.


---

## VI. The table: glass, water, order


A pairing table is a small ritual, not a stage for ego.

### Setting

- A glass fit for the drink (snifter, Glencairn, copita), water, ashtray, cutter.
- Do not warm brandy over a flame. Choose ice deliberately, not by habit.
- Pour modestly. Top up when the guest's glass runs low, without interrogation.

### Order

- Nose the drink, sip, smoke, sip again. Look for a bridge, not a victory.
- Milder first, then stronger, in both cigar and glass when you lead the evening.
- If a guest lags, slow your pace. Do not pull ahead alone.

### Words at the table

- Praising another's choice is manners. Ranking 'mine is better' is not.


---

## VII. Host and guest


A good host offers a ladder. A good guest knows how to step off it without shame.

### Host

- Ask about strength, smoke and time before you open the box.
- Keep a mild and a medium cigar plus an approachable drink (VSOP, tawny; not a peat bomb at the start).
- Show where the ashtray and the washroom are. Small things save an evening.
- Do not force 'one more' when the guest is full.

### Guest

- Bring a small gift if custom invites it (a bottle, a box, nothing excessive).
- Respect RH and order in the humidor. Do not rummage the host's stock.
- Thank specifically ('that amontillado with the Connecticut…').
- Shortening the final third is not weakness. It is measure.

### When something goes wrong

- Canoeing, a spill, a bad cut: smile, fix it quietly, keep the conversation.


---

## VIII. Outdoors and in passing


Wind and passers-by change the rules faster than a humidor.

### Outdoors

- Back to the wind. Do not send smoke into faces at the table.
- Keep a jet torch clear of hair, napkins and dry plants.
- Carry a travel ashtray or tin. Ash does not belong on the restaurant lawn.
- A panatela in wind wants shelter. Do not blame the format; adjust the place.

### In passing

- On the street, give a wide berth to doorways and waiting queues.
- In a taxi or someone else's car, only with clear consent.
- Balconies above others' windows: smoke rises. Choose hour and direction.

### Public space

- Where smoking is allowed, measure still applies. Where it is not, it is finished.


---

## IX. Gift, thanks, remembrance


A good gift opens conversation. It does not close it with a price tag.

### Giving

- Cigars: a few in tubes or a small box, with a note on format and strength.
- Drink: a bottle you would drink yourself. Avoid 'most expensive without context'.
- Tools: a cutter or glass they will use, not decor that gathers dust.

### Thanks

- A message the next day: short and specific.
- Return the invitation when you can. Manners are a circle, not an invoice.

### Remembrance

- Note what worked (vitola, drink, bridge). The app Collection serves that too.
- Do not retell another's table weakness as an anecdote outside.


---

## X. Last word


If you remember only three things, let them be these.

### Three table rules

- Ask before the flame.
- Leave room in the smoke, in the glass and in the talk.
- Leave cleaner than you found it: ash, glass, thanks.

### Closing

- Manners are learned like pairing: by notes, by repetition, without theatre.
- The best compliment is not 'you know the rules'. It is 'I was at ease with you'.
