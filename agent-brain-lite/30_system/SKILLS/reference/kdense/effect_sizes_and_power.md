# Effect sizes and power

## Always report with inferential tests

- Group comparisons: Cohen's d or Hedges g (CI).
- ANOVA: eta-squared or partial eta-squared.
- Regression: R² / adjusted R²; standardized betas where helpful.
- Logistic: odds ratio with 95% CI.
- Survival: hazard ratio with 95% CI.

## Interpretation anchors (Cohen, conventional)

| Measure | Small | Medium | Large |
|---------|-------|--------|-------|
| d | 0.2 | 0.5 | 0.8 |
| eta² | .01 | .06 | .14 |

Clinical importance may differ from statistical thresholds; separate statistical vs practical significance in Discussion.

## Power and sample size

- A priori power for primary outcome when protocol allows.
- Post-hoc power on non-significant results is discouraged; prefer CI width and precision.
- Underpowered studies: report imprecision; avoid "no effect" language.

## Multiple testing

Pre-specify correction in SAP. Bonferroni (conservative), Holm, or FDR for exploratory sets.
