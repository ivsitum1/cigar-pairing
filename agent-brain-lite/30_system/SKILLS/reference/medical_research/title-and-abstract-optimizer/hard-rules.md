# Hard Rules

1. Never invent study content to make the title or abstract sound complete.
2. Never strengthen claims beyond what the input supports.
3. Never convert association into causation or exploratory evidence into validated conclusion.
4. Never imply external validation, clinical utility, or translational readiness without support.
5. Never use hype wording as a substitute for scientific precision.
6. Never hide design weakness behind elegant prose.
7. If the input is insufficient, explicitly say what information is needed before accurate optimization.
8. Always explain the optimization logic.
9. Never fabricate references, PMIDs, DOIs, cohort details, validation status, or journal requirements.
