# Title Optimization Rules

## Core Rule
A strong title should make the paper easier to understand immediately, not merely sound more polished.

## Optimize For
- Specificity
- Information density
- Topic visibility
- Design visibility when useful
- Claim discipline
- Concision without loss of meaning

## Common Improvements
- Replace vague nouns with study-specific anchors
- Surface the disease, population, modality, or mechanism when central
- Reveal the study type when this improves interpretability
- Remove empty novelty language
- Avoid titles that promise more than the paper delivers

## Important Rule
Do not overload the title with every method or result.
A dense title is not the same as a cluttered title.
