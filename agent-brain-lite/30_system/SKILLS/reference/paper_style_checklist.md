# Paper Style Checklist (All Manuscript Types)

**Source:** Original position-paper style rules + `58_manuscript_agent_protocol.md` Part 3  
**Companion:** `.cursor/rules/writing-paper-style.mdc`

---

## 1. Tone — assertive, not defensive

- Take a clear stance; this is not a neutral review.
- Limitations **once** (Limitations subsection), not in every paragraph.
- Audit hedging: `may`, `might`, `partly`, `potentially` — keep only if truth requires it.
- After Abstract, reader knows **what you argue and why**.

## 2. Redundancy

- Each claim once, in the right section.
- Key thesis in Abstract and Conclusion; each section adds a **new** dimension.
- If paraphrasing the same conclusion — delete or replace with new evidence.

## 3. Voice — clinical, not bureaucratic

- Active for recommendations: "We recommend", "Centres should", "The evidence supports".
- Passive in Methods/Results where conventional.
- "In our practice" where author voice fits (position paper, case series, expert narrative).

## 4. Proportionality

| Budget | ~Share | IMRaD mapping |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Core argument / findings | 60% | Results + Discussion |
| Background | 20% | Introduction |
| Technical digressions | 10% | Methods footnotes, brief digressions + refs |
| Limitations + future | 10% | Limitations, Conclusion |

## 5. Tables vs text

- Table = scan summary.
- Text = interpretation, context, clinical implication.
- Never repeat table data verbatim in prose.

## 6. Sentence structure

- Target ≤25 words per sentence (guideline, not rigid rule).
- One idea = one sentence.
- References at end of sentence, not nested mid-clause.
- Avoid triple-negative constructs.

## 7. Precision over diplomacy

- Prefer: "X reduced Y by 60% versus Z (25% vs 62.5%)"
- Avoid: "X was associated with lower rates of Y compared with Z in one trial" when numbers exist.
- **Honesty:** direct language only when evidence and design support it.

## 8. Self-check

- Paragraph readable without prior 3 paragraphs?
- Abstract alone: what, why, what now?

---

## Examples by type

| Type | Assertive voice lives in |
|------|-------------------------|
| RCT | Discussion, Conclusions (Results = facts) |
| Cohort | Discussion, clinical implications |
| Position paper | Abstract, Discussion, Conclusions |
| Narrative review | Synthesis sections, recommendations |
