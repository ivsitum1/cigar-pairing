## Voice contract

### Bridges (primary)

- Short scenes. One situation, one clear move.
- Warm, slightly wry, never scolding. The reader is an ally, not a pupil.
- Prefer “gospodin čini X” / concrete action over abstract virtue lists.
- Precept rhythm OK; telegram bullets (“Krivo: / Ljubazno:”) only if the user asks for a card format.
- End scenes with what to *do*, not with a moral lecture.

### Sinek (light, secondary)

- One breath of *why* before the how: evening = shared trust, not performance.
- Purpose stays human: others breathe easier; belonging at the table.
- Never TED-speak, never “start with why” as a slogan, never corporate mission tone.

### Hard bans (this project)

| Avoid | Prefer |
|-------|--------|
| vinjeta / mini-vinjeta | prizor, scena, mala priča, situacija, trenutak |
| situacija → krivo → ljubazno (as default scaffold) | continuous prose scene |
| em dash (—) | comma, colon, or two sentences |
| AI stock (delve, tapestry, pivotal, seamless…) | concrete Croatian nouns and verbs |
| anglizmi and calques when a clear HR word exists | hrvatski standard; keep brand/product names as needed |

## Chapter / scene workflow

1. **Purpose (one line):** What must the reader believe after this piece?
2. **Hook:** concrete moment at the table (sight, sound, social friction).
3. **Why (one sentence max):** what the evening loses if this goes wrong.
4. **Move:** what a gentleman does (quiet, specific, sayable aloud).
5. **Close:** forward pull, not “u zaključku”.

Target length per scene: ~80–140 words. Ten scenes ≈ one readable chapter, not a checklist wall.

### Self-check before delivery

- [ ] Would Bridges recognize the rhythm (short, courteous, actionable)?
- [ ] Is there a quiet *why* without sermon?
- [ ] Zero “vinjeta”; HR vocabulary reads natural aloud?
- [ ] No Krivo/Ljubazno telegraph unless requested?
- [ ] Varied sentence length; no em dash; no AI filler?
